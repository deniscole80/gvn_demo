<p>A year old baby Ademola Adekoya Urgently Seeks $400 For Hole in the Heart Surgery Medical Bills Clearance at Babcock University Teaching Hospital, Nigeria. Baby Adekoya was diagnosed with Ventricular Septal Defect (VSD) after multiple tests were carried out, as shown in the documents made available.</p> <p>According to research, ventricular septal defect is a common heart defect most often present at birth, but it can occur in adults after surgery or a heart attack. It involves a hole in the wall between the heart’s lower chambers.</p> <p>Shortly after his birth January 2021, baby Ademola was said to have fallen sick and was taken to the General Hospital Ikorodu, Lagos, Nigeria for a medical check. What the parents thought was a minor baby illness turned out to be VSD.</p> <p>Speaking to Gifting Volunteer Network, the mother, Mrs. Hannah Adekoya, narrated that when she took her baby to the hospital, the doctor told her that her son had an infection. “So, they placed him on drips and medication. “The doctor checked him again and noticed he had mono in his chest. So the doctor recommended an X-ray. We did it. In the report of the X-ray it showed he has Cevenicat disease.</p> <p>After that, the doctor recommended an echocardiogram for the baby at the Afriglobal Facility. ”She got a call from the Ikorodu General Hospital that the baby be referred to LASUTH Ayike House since General Hospital does not attend to such cases.</p> <p>“His mother said when they got to Lagos State University Teaching Hospital (LASUTH), I was able to see a cardiologist. They gave me an appointment to come every three months. I used to go during the appointed time. “Last month, they called and said the hole in my baby could not be closed, that the baby is having VSD and that the hole is of moderate size.</p> <p>The Dr. said the Hole can only be closed through surgery. They told me the bill is in millions of naira – N5 million($10000).” And with the options that the surgery could only be done at Babcock University Teaching Hospital, Lagos Nigeria or India. Mrs. Adekoya took her baby to Babcock University Teaching Hospital where the Surgery was done and a Sum of $9600 was paid for the surgery, now $400 left to clear Ademola Surgery Bills to make enquiries.</p> <p>Please we need your assistance to clear my baby medical bills hanging at the Hospital.</p> <p>We’ve spent our last savings and sold valuable items for his surgery at Babcock University Teaching Hospital but now stranded with no option than to seek for the General Populace for assistance.</p> <p>My husband is working in a small firm with a salary which can only carter for family upkeep. It is difficult to eat twice a day.</p> <p>We lost our jobs in the process trying to save our baby’s life and things became hard after paying $9600 for the Surgery.</p> <p>Please help us God bless you</p>
